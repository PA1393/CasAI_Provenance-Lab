# Base editing in crops

## Mechanism

- CBE converts C·G to T·A; ABE converts A·T to G·C — installing point mutations without a double-strand break
- The editing window is roughly protospacer positions 4-8; guide placement controls bystander edits

## Why it matters for crops

- A single precise base change can recreate a favorable natural allele (e.g. herbicide-tolerance mutations in ALS or EPSPS)
- Lower indel risk than nuclease editing, which can mean a cleaner regulatory profile in some jurisdictions
